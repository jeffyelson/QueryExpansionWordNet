The Cordillera Occidental (English: West Andes) is the lowest in elevation of the three branches of the Colombian Andes.  The average altitude is 2,000 m (6,600 ft) and the highest peak is Cerro Tatamá at 4,100 m (13,500 ft).  The range extends from south to north dividing from the Colombian Massif in Nariño Department, passes north through Cauca, Valle del Cauca, Risaralda, Chocó, and Caldas Departments to the Paramillo Massif in Antioquia and Córdoba Departments.  From this massif the range divides further to form the Serranías de Ayapel, San Jerónimo and Abibe.  Only to recede into the Caribbean plain and the Sinú River valley.
